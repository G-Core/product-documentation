Gcore Colocation places customer-owned servers and network hardware in enterprise-grade data centers. It combines the reliability of Tier 3 and Tier 4 certified facilities with direct access to Gcore's global network and built-in DDoS protection, with 24/7 on-site technical support included in every plan.

## Rack options

Two configurations are available across all 15 Gcore colocation locations.

| Option | Space | Committed power |
|--------|-------|-----------------|
| Full rack | 1 dedicated rack | 5 kW |
| Rack unit | 1 RU in a shared rack | 500 W |

A full rack provides an isolated, dedicated space suited for larger deployments. A rack unit fits a smaller footprint or an initial deployment within a shared rack.

## Included services

Every plan includes the following services regardless of rack type or location.

### Power and cooling

Each rack includes redundant power at the committed capacity and active cooling managed by the data center facility.

### Network connectivity

Colocated equipment connects directly to Gcore's global low-latency network, built for content delivery and high-performance applications. High-bandwidth internet access is part of the plan at every location.

### DDoS protection

Traffic to colocated servers can be routed through Gcore's own scrubbing infrastructure before reaching the equipment. This is a native network capability — not a third-party service — and it's included at no additional cost.

### On-site technical support

Technical staff are on-site 24/7 to handle remote hands, hardware troubleshooting, racking and stacking, and emergency response. For more complex hands-on work — hardware installation, component replacement, diagnostics, or cable management — the [Smart Hands](/colocation/smart-hands) service is available as a paid add-on.

## Data center tiers

Gcore colocates only in the same Tier 3 and Tier 4 facilities used for its own mission-critical infrastructure. Both tiers are certified under the Uptime Institute standard.

| Tier | Uptime | Redundancy model |
|------|--------|-----------------|
| Tier 3 | 99.982% | N+1 |
| Tier 4 | 99.995% | 2N fully fault-tolerant |

Tier 3 facilities provide concurrent maintainability — hardware can be serviced without taking the system offline. Tier 4 adds full fault tolerance, meaning any single component failure does not interrupt operations.
