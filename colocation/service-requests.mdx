Colocation customers submit service requests to have Gcore's on-site technical staff perform physical tasks at the data center on their behalf. Every service request is a Smart Hands task and is billed on a time and materials basis.

## Request types

Five types of service requests are available, each covering a distinct category of on-site activity.

### Data center access

Data center access requests authorize a customer representative to enter the facility and work directly on their equipment. Contact [support](https://support.gcore.com) to arrange access in advance.

### Parcel reception

Parcel reception requests instruct Smart Hands staff to receive a hardware shipment on the customer's behalf when it arrives at the data center. The shipment is accepted, logged, and held until the customer arranges installation or pickup.

### Equipment operations

Equipment operations requests cover hands-on work performed by Smart Hands engineers on customer hardware already installed in the facility. Tasks include basic checks (server reboots, indicator light verification, cable inspection) as well as more complex work such as hardware installation, component replacement, and cable management.

### Equipment dispatch

Equipment dispatch requests arrange for Smart Hands staff to decommission and prepare customer equipment for shipment out of the facility. Staff coordinate the packaging and handoff to the carrier or the customer's representative.

### Letter of Authorization

A Letter of Authorization (LOA) request produces a formal document authorizing a named third party to access the customer's colocated equipment on the customer's behalf. The facility requires an LOA before granting access to any third-party representative.

## Priorities and response times

Each request is assigned one of two priority levels, which determines the response commitment and the billing rate. Time is billed in 15-minute increments, rounded up, calculated separately per priority type and per location.

| Priority | Scope | Response commitment |
|----------|-------|---------------------|
| Standard | Requests completable during business hours | Best effort |
| High | Urgent requests and requests outside business hours | Engineer starts work the next business day |

Priority is set by the customer when submitting through the support portal. For requests submitted by chat or email, the support team assigns a priority based on the request details.

## Submit a request

Requests can be submitted through the Customer Portal, by live chat, or by email.

- Open a [support ticket](https://support.gcore.com) and select the request type and priority.
- Use the live chat in the Customer Portal; the support team assigns a priority based on the request details.
- Send a request description by email to Gcore support; the support team assigns a priority based on the description.
