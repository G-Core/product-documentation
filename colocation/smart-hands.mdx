Smart Hands is a paid on-site technical support service for Gcore Colocation customers. Data center engineers perform physical tasks directly on customer hardware inside the facility. It's available when on-site intervention is needed but the customer's own team is not on-site.

## What Smart Hands covers

Smart Hands engineers perform physical tasks that require hands-on access to customer hardware at the data center. Work covered includes:

- Hardware installation and removal (servers, switches, drives, and other components)
- Component replacement (failed or degraded parts)
- Cabling and cable management
- Server reboots and power cycling
- Indicator light and status checks
- Visual inspection and basic diagnostics
- Any other physical task at the rack that requires on-site presence

Smart Hands does not cover work that requires the customer's own credentials or software access to the equipment.

## Priority tiers

Two priority levels are available. The customer specifies the priority when submitting the request.

| Priority | Scope | SLA |
|----------|-------|-----|
| Standard | Non-urgent work completable during business hours | Best effort |
| High | Urgent work or work required outside business hours | Engineer starts the next business day |

If the customer doesn't specify a priority, the support team assigns one based on the request details.

## Billing

Smart Hands time is billed per hour, in 15-minute increments rounded up. Standard and high-priority work are billed at different rates. Time is calculated separately per data center location and per priority type.

For example: two tasks at the same location taking 5 and 30 minutes produce a combined 35 minutes, which rounds up to 45 minutes billed for that location. If one of those tasks was submitted as high priority, the high-priority time is calculated separately from the standard time.

Rates vary by location. Contact the [sales team](https://gcore.com/contact-sales) for pricing at your specific facility.

## Submit a request

Every Smart Hands request is submitted as a service request. For available request channels and how to submit, see [Service requests](/colocation/service-requests).
