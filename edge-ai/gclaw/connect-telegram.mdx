# Connect Telegram to Gclaw

This guide explains how to create a Telegram bot and connect it to your Gclaw instance, allowing you to interact with your AI assistant directly from Telegram.

<Tip>
You can also ask your Gclaw assistant directly: "How do I connect Telegram?" and follow the interactive instructions in the chat.
</Tip>

## Prerequisites

Before you begin, ensure you have:

- A Gclaw instance running. See [Getting started](/edge-ai/gclaw/getting-started).
- A Telegram account.

## Step 1: Create a Telegram bot

To create a bot and get your bot token:

1. Open Telegram and search for **@BotFather**.

2. Start a chat with BotFather and send the command:
   ```
   /newbot
   ```

3. Follow the prompts to configure your bot:
   - Enter a display name for your bot (for example, "My Gclaw Assistant")
   - Enter a username for your bot (must end with "bot", for example, "my_gclaw_bot")

4. After successful creation, BotFather will send you a message containing your bot token. The token looks like:
   ```
   123456789:ABCdefGHIjklMNOpqrSTUvwxYZ
   ```

5. Copy and save this token. You will need it in the next step.

<Warning>
Keep your bot token secure. Anyone with access to this token can control your bot.
</Warning>

## Step 2: Add the token to OpenClaw

To configure your Gclaw instance with the Telegram bot token:

1. Open your Gclaw chat interface in the Gcore Customer Portal.

2. In the chat, ask your assistant to help configure Telegram, or run the following command in your terminal:

```bash
openclaw config set channels.telegram.botToken "YOUR_TOKEN_HERE"
```

Replace `YOUR_TOKEN_HERE` with the token you received from BotFather.

3. The gateway will automatically reload with your new bot configuration.

## Step 3: Connect your Telegram account

Gclaw uses pairing mode by default to connect your Telegram account:

1. Open Telegram and find your newly created bot by searching for its username.

2. Send any message to your bot (for example, "Hello").

3. Your bot will respond with a pairing code that looks like:
   ```
   openclaw pairing approve telegram ABC123
   ```

4. Run the provided command in your terminal to approve the pairing.

## Verify the connection

After completing the pairing:

1. Send a test message to your bot in Telegram.

2. Your Gclaw assistant should respond to your message.

If you do not receive a response, check that:
- Your bot token is correct
- The pairing was completed successfully
- Your Gclaw instance is running

## Advanced configuration

You can customize your Telegram integration by asking your Gclaw assistant:

- "How do I set up group support for Telegram?"
- "Configure my Telegram bot to require @mentions in groups"
- "How do I allow multiple accounts on my Telegram bot?"

Your assistant will provide specific configuration commands for each option.

## Troubleshooting

If you encounter issues:

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Bot not responding | Verify your bot token is correct and the instance is running |
| Pairing code not received | Ensure you are messaging the correct bot username |
| Configuration not applying | Try restarting your instance from the Settings panel |

For additional help, contact [Gcore support](mailto:support@gcore.com).
