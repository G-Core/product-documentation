# Connect Discord to Gclaw

This guide explains how to create a Discord bot application and connect it to your Gclaw instance, allowing you to interact with your AI assistant in Discord servers.

## Prerequisites

Before you begin, ensure you have:

- A Gclaw instance running. See [Getting started](/edge-ai/gclaw/getting-started).
- A Discord account.
- Administrator access to a Discord server where you want to add the bot.

## Step 1: Create a Discord bot

To create a bot application and get your bot token:

1. Go to the [Discord Developer Portal](https://discord.com/developers/applications).

2. Click **New Application**.

3. Enter a name for your application (for example, "Gclaw Bot") and click **Create**.

4. In the left sidebar, click **Bot**.

5. Click **Add Bot**, then confirm by clicking **Yes, do it!**.

6. Under **Privileged Gateway Intents**, enable the following option:
   - **Message Content Intent** - Required to read message content

7. Click **Reset Token**, then click **Copy** to copy your bot token.

<Warning>
Keep your bot token secure. Anyone with access to this token can control your bot. If your token is compromised, reset it immediately in the Developer Portal.
</Warning>

## Step 2: Add the token to OpenClaw

To configure your Gclaw instance with the Discord bot token:

1. Open your Gclaw chat interface in the Gcore Customer Portal.

2. In the chat, ask your assistant to help configure Discord, or run the following command in your terminal:

```bash
openclaw config set channels.discord.botToken "YOUR_TOKEN_HERE"
```

Replace `YOUR_TOKEN_HERE` with the token you copied from the Developer Portal.

3. The gateway will automatically reload with your new bot configuration.

## Step 3: Invite the bot to your server

To add your bot to a Discord server:

1. In the Discord Developer Portal, navigate to your application.

2. In the left sidebar, click **OAuth2** > **URL Generator**.

3. Under **Scopes**, select **bot**.

4. Under **Bot Permissions**, select the following permissions:
   - **Read Messages/View Channels**
   - **Send Messages**
   - **Read Message History**

5. Copy the generated URL at the bottom of the page.

6. Open the URL in your browser.

7. Select the server where you want to add the bot and click **Authorize**.

8. Complete any verification prompts.

## Verify the connection

After adding the bot to your server:

1. Go to a text channel where the bot has access.

2. Send a test message mentioning the bot or addressing it directly.

3. Your Gclaw assistant should respond to your message.

If you do not receive a response, check that:
- Your bot token is correct
- The bot has the required permissions in the channel
- The Message Content Intent is enabled
- Your Gclaw instance is running

## Advanced configuration

You can customize your Discord integration with additional options:

### Mention requirements

To configure the bot to only respond when mentioned:
- Ask your assistant: "Configure Discord to require @mentions"

### Threading behavior

To enable the bot to respond in threads:
- Ask your assistant: "How do I enable threading for Discord?"

### Role-based access

To restrict bot access to specific roles:
- Ask your assistant: "How do I set up role-based access for Discord?"

## Required permissions summary

| Permission | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| Read Messages/View Channels | Allows the bot to see channels and read messages |
| Send Messages | Allows the bot to send responses |
| Read Message History | Allows the bot to read previous messages for context |

## Troubleshooting

If you encounter issues:

1. **Bot not responding**: Verify your bot token is correct and the Message Content Intent is enabled.

2. **Bot cannot see messages**: Check that the bot has the Read Messages permission in the channel.

3. **Permission errors**: Re-invite the bot using a URL with all required permissions selected.

4. **Configuration not applying**: Try restarting your instance from the Settings panel.

For additional help, contact [Gcore support](mailto:support@gcore.com).
