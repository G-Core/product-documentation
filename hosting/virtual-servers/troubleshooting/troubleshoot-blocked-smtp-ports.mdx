SMTP ports 25, 465, and 587 are blocked when the system detects unusual outbound email traffic from a virtual server. This is a spam prevention measure.

## Verify the block

To verify whether the ports were blocked, navigate to **Support** > **Support tickets** in the [Hosting&nbsp;portal](https://hosting.gcore.com/billmgr). A blocking notification appears as a ticket with the subject "SMTP ports have been blocked" in the list.

<Frame>![Support tickets section in the Hosting portal sidebar, showing the empty ticket list](/images/docs/hosting/virtual-servers/troubleshooting/troubleshoot-blocked-smtp-ports/troubleshoot-blocked-smtp-ports-image1.png)</Frame>

## Blocking policy

Per paragraph two (d) of the [usage&nbsp;policy](https://gcore.com/legal), servers cannot be used to send unsolicited emails, chain letters, or spam. Outbound email is restricted to users of services hosted on Gcore servers.

## Request unblocking

[Contact&nbsp;support](/hosting/contact-our-technical-support) and include the details that match the situation:

| Scenario | Provide |
|---|---|
| Legitimate email was sent | The domain name of the service whose users received the mailing |
| No mailing was sent, but blocking notifications appeared | The server setting that caused excessive SMTP traffic and how it was fixed |
| No mailing was sent, no notifications appeared, but ports do not work | Confirm that no blocking notification was received and describe any recent network configuration changes |
