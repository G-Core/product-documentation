SMTP ports 25, 465, and 587 are blocked when the system detects unusual outbound email traffic from a virtual server. This is a spam prevention measure.

To check for a blocking notification, navigate to **Support** > **Support tickets** in the [Hosting&nbsp;portal](https://hosting.gcore.com/billmgr). A blocking notification appears as a ticket with the subject "SMTP ports have been blocked" in the list.

Per paragraph 2 (d) of the [Acceptable Use Policy](https://gcore.com/legal), servers cannot be used to send unsolicited emails, chain letters, or spam. Outbound email is restricted to users of services hosted on Gcore servers.

To request unblocking, [contact&nbsp;support](/hosting/contact-our-technical-support) and include the details that match the situation:

| Situation | Include in the support request |
|---|---|
| Legitimate email was sent | The domain name of the service whose users received the mailing |
| No mailing was sent, but blocking notifications appeared | The server setting that caused excessive SMTP traffic and how it was fixed |
| No mailing was sent, no notifications appeared, but ports do not work | Confirmation that no blocking notifications were received and whether network settings were changed |
