A virtual server is suspended when the account balance falls below the minimum required to cover the renewal. Billing continues to accrue during the suspension period.

## Signs of non-payment suspension

Two indicators appear when a server is suspended for non-payment:

- The server's **Service status** shows **Suspended** in the server list.
- On the dashboard, the **Minimum amount for service activation** value exceeds the current **Account balance**.

## Reactivate the server

There are two ways to reactivate a suspended server.

### Recharge the account balance

When funds are added to the account balance, the system automatically applies them to unpaid services and reactivates the server immediately.

1. On the dashboard, click **Add funds** in the **Quick access** section.

<Frame>
  ![Dashboard Quick access section with the Add funds button](/images/docs/hosting/virtual-servers/troubleshooting/troubleshoot-a-server-that-was-suspended-for-non-payment-/troubleshoot-a-server-that-was-suspended-for-non-payment-image3.png)
</Frame>

2. Select a [payment&nbsp;method](/hosting/payments/pay-for-gcore-services-payment-methods) and complete the payment.

If the funds are not credited within 15 minutes (payment system) or five business days (bank transfer), contact [support](/hosting/contact-our-technical-support).

### Replenish the linked card

When [auto&nbsp;payment](/hosting/payments/set-up-auto-payment) is enabled, the system first deducts from the account balance and — if the balance is empty — charges the linked card. If both are empty, services are suspended. The system retries the charge 10, 5, 4, 3, 2, 1, and 0 days before the services expire.

Add funds to the linked card — the next automatic retry will charge the card, credit the account balance, and reactivate the server.

## Verify the payment

Track the payment status in the **Billing** section depending on the method used.

### Manual balance recharge

Track the payment in **Billing** > **Payments**. When a payment is initiated, the system creates a record with **New** status. Once the funds reach the account, the status changes to **Paid**.

<Frame>
  ![Payments section showing two payments with Paid status](/images/docs/hosting/virtual-servers/troubleshooting/troubleshoot-a-server-that-was-suspended-for-non-payment-/troubleshoot-a-server-that-was-suspended-for-non-payment-image4.png)
</Frame>

### Auto payment charge

Track the charge in **Billing** > **Expenses**. Each charge attempt creates a row. When the charge succeeds, the **Not paid** column shows zero and the **Payments** column shows the corresponding payment number.

<Frame>
  ![Expenses section showing rows with zero Not paid values after successful charges](/images/docs/hosting/virtual-servers/troubleshooting/troubleshoot-a-server-that-was-suspended-for-non-payment-/troubleshoot-a-server-that-was-suspended-for-non-payment-image5.png)
</Frame>

If the funds do not appear on the balance within 15 minutes, contact [support](/hosting/contact-our-technical-support).

## Payment timing

Crediting times depend on the payment method.

- **Card charge:** funds are credited to the balance within 15 minutes.
- **Bank transfer:** funds are credited within five business days.

The server is reactivated as soon as the payment covers the outstanding balance.

## Server deletion after non-payment

If the server remains in **Stopped** status for 14 days, it is automatically deleted and all data is permanently erased. KVM-SSD-1 and KVM-SAS-1 servers are deleted one day after non-payment.
